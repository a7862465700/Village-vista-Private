# Village Springs Land Co. — Brand Identity Guide

## Brand Overview

**Village Springs Land Co.** is a vacant land wholesale company based in Arkansas. We help homesteaders, builders, and families find affordable land where they can put down roots and build the life they want.

Our name draws from the idea of a village — a close community built on shared ground — and natural springs, the kind of clean, flowing water that runs through the Arkansas landscape.

## Mission, Vision & Values

### Mission
To make land ownership simple, affordable, and accessible for families who want to build something real.

### Vision
We see a world where owning land is not reserved for the wealthy or well-connected. Every family deserves a piece of ground to call their own — whether they want to build a cabin, grow a garden, or just know they have a place that belongs to them.

### Core Values

**Honesty First** — We tell you exactly what a parcel is and what it is not. No hidden surprises, no pressure. If a piece of land is not right for you, we will say so.

**Rooted in Community** — Land is not just dirt and trees. It is where families gather, where neighbors become friends, and where communities grow. We think about the people, not just the parcels.

**Keep It Simple** — Buying land should not feel like decoding a legal textbook. We make the process clear, straightforward, and human.

**Stewardship** — We respect the land we sell. Every parcel has a story, and we want to match it with someone who will take care of it.

## Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement
For homesteaders and families looking to own land in Arkansas, Village Springs Land Co. is the vacant land company that makes buying your own piece of ground feel personal, simple, and affordable — because we believe everyone deserves a place to put down roots.

### What Makes Us Different
- **Human-first communication:** We talk to buyers like neighbors, not salespeople.
- **Arkansas-rooted expertise:** We know the land, the counties, and the communities firsthand.
- **No-pressure approach:** We provide all the details upfront and let buyers decide on their timeline.
- **Affordable owner financing:** We work with buyers to offer payment plans that make sense for real families.

### Target Audience

**Primary:** Homesteaders, off-grid builders, and families aged 28–55 who are ready to own land but may not have huge upfront capital. They value self-sufficiency, nature, and building something with their own hands. They spend time on Facebook, Instagram, and YouTube researching land, tiny homes, and homesteading lifestyles.

**Secondary:** Land investors and flippers looking for wholesale deals in Arkansas. They are deal-driven, move fast, and value clear property details and responsive communication.

## Brand Voice & Personality

The Village Springs voice should feel like talking to a knowledgeable friend who happens to sell land. We are warm without being cheesy, helpful without being pushy, and confident without being loud.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | We Sound Like | We Don't Sound Like |
|---|---|---|
| Warm | "We'd love to help you find the right piece of land." | "Submit your inquiry and an agent will be in touch." |
| Clear | "This 2-acre parcel has road access, electric nearby, and no HOA." | "Premium lot featuring enhanced infrastructure adjacency." |
| Grounded | "Here's what this land actually looks like and what you can do with it." | "Unlock your dream lifestyle today!" |
| Encouraging | "Owning land is more doable than you think. Let us show you." | "Act now or miss out forever!" |

### Writing Rules

**Do:**
- Use first person ("we") and second person ("you") to keep things personal.
- Write in short, clear sentences. If a 12-year-old could not understand it, simplify.
- Mention specific parcel details (acreage, county, access, zoning) in every listing.

**Don't:**
- Use jargon like "turnkey investment opportunity" or "capitalize on appreciation."
- Use urgency pressure like "Only 1 left!" or "Price goes up tomorrow!"
- Use ALL CAPS for emphasis. Let the content speak for itself.

## Color Palette

### Primary Colors
- **Forest Green** — #2D5E3A — Primary brand color. Use for headlines, buttons, and the logo.
- **Sage Green** — #6B9B7D — Supporting green for accents and secondary elements.
- **Warm Tan** — #C4A882 — Use for "LAND CO." text, dividers, and warm accents.

### Secondary Colors
- **Spring Teal** — #7FBBAD — Accent for links, highlights, and secondary CTAs.
- **Cream** — #F5F0E8 — Background color for a warm, inviting feel.
- **Warm Brown** — #8B7355 — Subheadings and tertiary text.
- **Charcoal** — #2C2C2C — Body text. Avoid pure black (#000000).

### Color Usage Rules
- Forest Green is the primary brand color. Use it for headlines, buttons, and the logo.
- Cream works best as a background color for a warm, inviting feel.
- Charcoal is for body text. Avoid pure black — it feels too harsh.
- Spring Teal is an accent for links, highlights, and secondary CTAs.
- Never place light text on light backgrounds. Always check contrast for readability.

## Typography

### Primary Typefaces

**Headlines & Titles:** Georgia (serif) — Use for all headings, page titles, and display text. Bold weight for H1 and H2, regular for H3.

**Body & UI Text:** Arial (sans-serif) — Use for body copy, captions, buttons, and all UI elements.

### Type Scale

| Element | Font | Size | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| H1 / Page Title | Georgia | 20pt | Bold |
| H2 / Section | Georgia | 15pt | Bold |
| H3 / Subsection | Arial | 12pt | Bold |
| Body Text | Arial | 11pt | Regular |
| Caption / Small | Arial | 9pt | Regular |
| Button / CTA | Arial | 11pt | Bold |

### Web Alternatives
For web and digital, substitute **Playfair Display** (Google Fonts) for headlines and **Lato** (Google Fonts) for body copy.

## Logo

The logo combines a mountain-and-spring icon with the brand name. Colors: dark green mountains, tan flowing line, teal spring water, with "Village Springs" in dark green serif and "LAND CO." in tan spaced sans-serif with flanking dashes.

### Clear Space & Minimum Size
- Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "V" in "Village" around all sides of the logo.
- Minimum width: 150px for digital, 1.5 inches for print.

### Logo Don'ts
- Do not stretch, skew, or rotate the logo.
- Do not change the logo colors outside the approved palette.
- Do not place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds.
- Do not add drop shadows, outlines, or effects.
- Do not rearrange the icon and text.

## Photography & Imagery

- Shoot in natural light whenever possible. Golden hour is best for land photos.
- Show the land from multiple angles: wide shot, close-ups of features, and neighboring context.
- Include people when possible — it gives scale and helps buyers picture themselves there.
- Keep editing warm and natural. Light warmth, slight contrast boost. Avoid heavy filters or HDR.
- Drone shots are gold. Aerial views with property lines overlaid are top-performing content.
- When enhancing images with AI tools, keep adjustments subtle. Never add structures or features that do not exist on the parcel.
